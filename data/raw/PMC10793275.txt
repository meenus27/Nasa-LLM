</div></section></section><section aria-label="Article content"><section class="body main-article-body"><section class="abstract" id="abstract1"><h2>ABSTRACT</h2>
<p>Sphingomonadaceae are common membrane colonizers and biofilm formers. As part of our studies on long-term genetic changes in drinking water biofilm species, we report the draft genome sequence of <em>Sphingomonas</em> strain Sph5, isolated from a tap water filtration membrane. The isolate was determined as <em>Sphingomonas paucimobilis</em> through whole genome sequencing and <em>de novo</em> assembly.</p>
<section id="kwd-group1" class="kwd-group"><p><strong>KEYWORDS:</strong> draft genome, WGS, water, <em>Sphingomonas</em></p></section></section><section id="s1"><h2 class="pmc_sec_title">ANNOUNCEMENT</h2>
<p>Widely used for drinking water, membrane filtration systems face biofouling due to microbe accumulation and biofilm formation. Studies reveal Sphingomonadaceae as common initial colonizers, persisting dominantly during biofilm growth (<a href="#B1" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B1">1</a>
<a href="#B2" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B2">–</a>
<a href="#B4" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B4">4</a>). Here, we report the genome sequence of a prevalent isolate.</p>
<p>
<em>Sphingomonas</em> spp. Sph5 was isolated from a biofilm on a Nadir MP005 microfiltration membrane used for drinking water biofouling studies (<a href="#B1" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B1">1</a>, <a href="#B5" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B5">5</a>). Sph5 was previously reported to be phylogenetically closest to <em>Sphingomonas sanguinis</em> strains BAB-7166 (99%) and NBRC 13937 (99%) based on 16S rRNA sequencing and cultivation methods (<a href="#B1" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B1">1</a>). However, the whole genome sequence (WGS) was not reported. WGS analysis is critical to uncover strain-specific traits associated with membrane biofouling. Here, we report the WGS of <em>Sphingomonas</em> spp. Sph5 using Illumina short-read sequencing and <em>de novo</em> assembly methods.</p>
<p>We received the <em>Sphingomonas</em> spp. Sph5 water isolate from Wetsus, Netherlands. Single isolated colonies were obtained on Reasoner’s 2A agar (R2A, Teknova R0005; Difco 214530) and cultured in R2A broth at room temperature. Genomic DNA was extracted using DNeasy UltraClean microbial kit (Qiagen 12224) following the company-provided protocol.</p>
<p>Sequencing libraries were generated using Kapa’s Hyperplus kit (KK8514) and IDT adapters (#00989130v2). Quality was verified using an Agilent Tapestation and qPCR (NEBNext Library Quant Kit, E7630L) on Thermo Fisher’s Quantstudio5.</p>
<p>Nineteen million 2 × 150 bp reads were obtained on a NovaSeq (Illumina) at Anschutz Medical Campus Genomics and Microarray Core, with an average Phred score of 33–36 calculated by FastQC [v0.11.9 (<a href="#B6" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B6">6</a>)].</p>
<p>Assembly was performed with SPADES, default isolate settings [v3.15.2 (<a href="#B7" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B7">7</a>)]. Contigs were aligned to NCBI databases with DIAMOND [v2.0.13 (<a href="#B8" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B8">8</a>)]. Completeness was estimated using BUSCO [v5.2.2 (<a href="#B9" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B9">9</a>)] proteobacteria marker genes. Of the 219 genes, there were 214 (97.7%) complete and single copy, 2 (0.9%) duplicated, 1 (0.5%) fragmented, and 2 (0.9%) missing.</p>
<p>For species identification, average nucleotide identity (ANI) was calculated between all complete <em>Sphingomonas</em> genomes and Sph5 with PYANI [v0.2.11 (<a href="#B10" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B10">10</a>)] and MUMMER [v4.0.0 (<a href="#B11" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B11">11</a>)]. ANI scores between Sph5 and <em>S. paucimobilis</em> ranged from 99.77%–99.95%, while ANI between Sph5 and <em>S. sanguinis</em> was 87.74%.</p>
<p>Annotations were predicted with Prokka [v1.14.6 (<a href="#B12" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B12">12</a>)], given <em>S. sanguinis</em> and <em>S. paucimobilis</em> references (<a href="#T1" class="usa-link">Table 1</a>), and used for orthogroup-based phylogeny construction with Orthofinder [v2.5.4 (<a href="#B13" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B13">13</a>)], MAFFT [v7.490 (<a href="#B14" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B14">14</a>)], and FastTree [v.2.1.10 (<a href="#B15" class="usa-link" aria-describedby="B15">15</a>)].</p>
